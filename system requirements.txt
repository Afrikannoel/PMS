Here’s a clear outline for both **functional** and **non-functional** requirements of the **Pharmacy Management System** (PMS) for your documentation:

### **Functional Requirements**
These define what the system will do—the core features and functions that the system must support.

1. **Drug Management:**
   - Allow the pharmacist to add new drugs to the system, specifying details such as name, price, quantity, supplier, and expiration date.
   - Update drug information and manage stock levels for existing drugs.
   - Track drugs by batch number for recall purposes.

2. **Sales and Transactions:**
   - Facilitate the sale of drugs, updating the database upon completion of each transaction.
   - Provide options to apply discounts to specific transactions.
   - Generate a receipt for each completed sale, with details on the drugs sold and total amount.

3. **Inventory Alerts:**
   - Alert the pharmacist when a drug’s stock is running low based on predefined thresholds.
   - Provide a weekly summary of drugs that are low in stock to assist with restocking.

4. **Reports Generation:**
   - Produce reports on drugs sold (daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly).
   - Generate reports on drugs that are low in stock.
   - Create profit and loss reports (daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly).
   - Generate reports on drugs in stock and those that are out of stock.

5. **Supplier Management:**
   - Store and manage details of drug suppliers, including contact information, pricing, and supply history.

6. **User Management:**
   - Support multiple users, each with unique credentials for logging in.
   - Implement role-based access control, with specific roles such as admin, pharmacist, and cashier.

7. **Customer and Prescription Management:**
   - Maintain customer records, including personal details and prescription history.
   - Allow pharmacists to manage and process prescriptions linked to specific customers.

8. **Return and Refund Handling:**
   - Handle drug returns, updating inventory and financial records accordingly.

9. **Drug Expiry Tracking:**
   - Track expiration dates of drugs and alert the pharmacist when a drug is nearing expiry.

10. **Audit Trails:**
    - Keep a record of all transactions and user activities in the system for auditing and accountability.

11. **Tax and Financial Calculations:**
    - Automatically calculate taxes and generate reports for tax purposes.

### **Non-Functional Requirements**
These define how the system will perform and the qualities it should have.

1. **Performance:**
   - The system should handle multiple transactions simultaneously without noticeable delays.
   - Generate reports in real-time with minimal latency.

2. **Scalability:**
   - The system should be able to scale to accommodate more users, drugs, and branches (if needed) as the pharmacy expands.

3. **Security:**
   - Data should be securely stored, with encryption for sensitive information such as user credentials and financial data.
   - Implement access control measures to restrict users based on their roles.

4. **Usability:**
   - The system should have an intuitive interface that is easy to navigate for users with minimal technical expertise.
   - Provide clear feedback (e.g., confirmation messages) on successful or failed actions.

5. **Reliability:**
   - Ensure the system is highly available with minimal downtime.
   - Include backup and recovery mechanisms to prevent data loss.

6. **Maintainability:**
   - The system should be easy to maintain, with clear documentation for future updates and modifications.
   - Ensure the system is modular, allowing for future enhancements without affecting the core functionality.

7. **Compliance:**
   - Ensure that the system complies with legal and industry regulations for drug handling, sales, and patient confidentiality (e.g., medical data protection standards).

8. **Data Integrity:**
   - Implement mechanisms to prevent duplicate entries and ensure accurate data is recorded for all transactions and reports.

9. **Availability:**
   - The system should be accessible during pharmacy business hours and support offline mode for local pharmacies with limited internet connectivity.

10. **Interoperability:**
    - Ensure that the system can integrate with third-party systems, such as payment gateways or external reporting tools if needed.
